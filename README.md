# Netflix Movies & TV Shows Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project delivers an interactive data visualization dashboard exploring the comprehensive catalog of **Netflix Movies and TV Shows**. The analysis uncovers trends in content acquisition over time, regional production powerhouses, distribution of content ratings, and the structural balance between film and episodic content on the platform.

## 📊 Key Insights & Platform Analytics
* **Content Mix:** Movies heavily dominate the platform's catalog, comprising **69.62% (6.13K)** of total content, compared to TV Shows at **30.38% (2.68K)**.
* **Acquisition Strategy:** Content addition experienced exponential growth post-2015, peaking sharply in **2019 with 1,999 titles added** in a single year, before seeing a slight strategic cooldown.
* **Target Audience Demographics:** **TV-MA (Mature Audiences)** and **TV-14** represent the most common content ratings by a wide margin, emphasizing Netflix's strong pivot toward adult and young-adult demographics.
* **Geographic Production Hubs:** The **United States** leads global content production with **2.8K titles**, followed closely by India (**1.0K titles**), highlighting the major hubs for Netflix originals and licensing.

## 🧩 Dashboard Features & Components
* **Core KPI Metrics:** Executive cards showcasing Total Movies (6.13K), Total Unique IDs (8.807K), Total Countries Involved (749), and Total TV Shows (2.676K).
* **Geographic Map/Bar Chart:** Identification of the *Top Content-Producing Countries*.
* **Audience Insights:** A frequency distribution bar chart illustrating the *Most Common Content Ratings*.
* **Temporal Trends:** * A smooth line chart tracking *Content Added by Year* to show platform growth.
   * An historical area/bar chart mapping *Total Show Release by Year* dating back to 1900.
* **Interactive Slicers:** Sidebar filter controls allowing users to switch between `Movie` and `TV Show` types or filter by specific `Years`.
